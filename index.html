    I’m a tech enthusiast, genuinely passionate about technology. I always want to explore and get my hands wet with new things, even if they are under development. My journey into the world of tech started back in middle school when I discovered it was possible to install custom ROMs, that is, alternative operating systems, on Android devices. This is how I got my phone broken once or twice, but the thrill of doing it was exhilarating. At high school, I was put into the International Baccalaureate program. I had noticed that one of the available courses was computer science, but my school didn’t have that class available. I was a bit upset about that, but I pursued other things and eventually went to university for economics. I found the world of self-learned programming in my last semester, as a bachelor’s student, and regained the passion for technology that I had. I also stumbled upon doomsday predictions about AI taking over software development entirely. I took two free courses from Harvard University, through EDX.org, and noticed, again, that it is still possible to gather the knowledge needed to break into the tech field. With this in mind, I applied for a second bachelor’s degree in computer science at Stockholm University. The learning journey goes on with the nature of technology, which is ever-changing. While AI might seem intimidating right now, I’m eager to adapt and excited about the future possibilities!
